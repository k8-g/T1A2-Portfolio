Portfolio Project

As part of doing the Coder Academy Bootcamp to study a Diploma of IT: Web Dev, we practised doing a basic website portfolio in class lessons, and then were tasked with making one for an assignment. In the basic portfolio, we were taught about HTML and how to style the HTML using CSS. I chose to use the code we were taught in class to expand on for my assignment.

Project code Project deployment

I built my website aimed at showcasing what I've learnt so far, as an aspiring web developer, with real work and education information, as well as allowing my personality to shine through my work, as I enjoy being expressive. Ideally, as I learn more, I can utilise this website as a real portfolio for when I eventually look for work when I have completed the course in its entirety.

Choosing the colour theme was the easiest part, as I chose my favourite colour that brings me joy. Admittedly, I wasn't sure how I was going to get it to look professional at first, but I hope I achieved that thus far. After doing up some basic wireframes for the mobile view, which I wanted to focus on first, I then started fluffing out the pages with test images and placeholder text to get the general look and layout sorted. After I had done that, I went back through, and focusing on a particular section or page, edited each to fit what I wanted.

I used Google a LOT to ask the questions of how to make this do that, or if I couldn't find exactly what I wanted in google, I tried different things out until it worked the way I wanted. This took up a huge chunk of my time, I would literaly stay up all night typing away, with music to keep me focused and frequent kisses on the cat's head to give me that little dopamine hit to keep going. Funnily enough, staying up all night got me into the zone more and I wanted to stay in the zone, as it took me a couple of hours after waking up to get back into it.

Kate Gerber 💜 2024
